[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
A passphrase is an optional 13th word or extended security layer that protects your seed phrase. In BIP39, it's commonly called the "25th word" (since the standard seed is 24 words).

Here's what you should know:

1. **What it does:** A passphrase creates a completely different wallet from the same seed phrase. Even if someone steals your 24-word seed, without the passphrase they can't access your funds.

2. **How it works:** When you combine your seed phrase + passphrase, it derives a different private key set. Same seed + different passphrase = different wallet entirely.

3. **Best practices:**
   - Use passphrases with hardware wallets (Ledger, Trezor) for maximum security
   - Never use dictionary words or predictable phrases
   - Store your passphrase securely and separately from your seed
   - Write it down if you have substantial funds
   - Test recovery with small amounts first to ensure you remember it exactly

4. **Critical:** If you're setting up a passphrase-protected wallet, use the vaultpilot-preflight skill (Step 0) before any derivation. It will verify consistency between your seed recovery and passphrase setup, and confirm you've tested the recovery path.

5. **Demo note:** If you're testing with a demo wallet, the preflight skill will guide you safely through the flow with checksums to prevent derivation errors.

Would you like help setting up a passphrase-protected wallet?

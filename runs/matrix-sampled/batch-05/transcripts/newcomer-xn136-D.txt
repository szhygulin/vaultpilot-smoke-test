[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Great question! "Points" in DeFi are a common gamification mechanism protocols use to reward users for participation. Here's what you need to know:

**What are DeFi points?**
Points are reward tokens or credits that protocols issue to users who perform specific actions—like providing liquidity, trading, lending, or just holding assets in a pool. They're usually non-transferable during the farming period.

**Why points and not real tokens?**
Protocols use points because they:
- Avoid regulatory complications (points aren't always classified as securities like tokens are)
- Build community engagement and loyalty before a token launch
- Let them distribute rewards fairly without immediately triggering dilution concerns
- Create measurable "proof of participation" for future airdrops

**How does point farming work?**
You typically:
1. Deposit assets into a protocol (liquidity, collateral, staking, etc.)
2. Earn points proportionally to your stake and time
3. Points accrue to your wallet address
4. Later, the protocol may "snapshot" your points and convert them 1:1 or at a ratio into actual tokens (the "airdrop")

**Why is everyone farming them?**
- **Airdrop speculation:** Users hope points convert to valuable tokens (like Arbitrum's airdrop from early users)
- **Low friction entry:** You're just depositing assets you'd probably hold anyway
- **Opportunity cost is minimal:** You're earning points on assets not doing much elsewhere

**The catch:**
- Points have zero value until converted—or might never convert if the protocol dissolves
- You're subject to smart contract risk the whole time
- High competition = points dilution (more farmers = smaller slice per person)
- Tax implications vary by jurisdiction; claiming airdrops can be a taxable event

Think of points like airline miles: they incentivize loyalty, build community data, and let the protocol launch with a committed user base rather than a cold token market.

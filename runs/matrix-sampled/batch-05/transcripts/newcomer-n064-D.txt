[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Putting BTC in a yield protocol is different from a traditional CD in a few key ways:

**Similarities:**
- Both lock up your assets for a period to earn returns
- Both offer predictable interest rates (though yield protocol rates can vary)

**Key Differences:**
- **Counterparty risk:** A CD is FDIC-insured (up to $250k in the US). A yield protocol relies on the protocol's smart contracts, which carry smart-contract risk, liquidation risk, and operational risk from the protocol team.
- **Liquidity:** Most CDs let you withdraw early (with a penalty). Yield protocols often have different unlock schedules — some let you exit anytime, others lock funds for a set period.
- **Volatility:** BTC itself is volatile. Even if the yield protocol is "safe," BTC's value can swing 10–20% in a week. A CD's principal is stable.
- **Regulation:** CDs are regulated banking products. Yield protocols operate in a less-regulated space; regulatory changes could affect them.

**Bottom line:** Yield protocols can be a way to earn on idle BTC, but they're much riskier than a CD. Only commit BTC you're comfortable losing. Start small, understand the protocol's mechanism, and watch for liquidation thresholds if borrowing is involved.

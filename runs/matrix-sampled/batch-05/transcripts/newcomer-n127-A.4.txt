[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Great question. Here's what to do if your hardware wallet stops working:

**Immediate steps:**
1. **Don't panic.** Your funds aren't lost—they're still on the blockchain, secured by your seed phrase.
2. **Locate your seed phrase.** This is typically a 12 or 24-word recovery phrase written down when you first set up the device.
3. **Verify you have it backed up.** Check your safe, safety deposit box, or whatever secure location you chose. This is critical.

**Recovery options:**
- **Replace the device.** Buy a new hardware wallet of the same brand (Ledger, Trezor, etc.) and restore using your seed phrase. This is the safest path.
- **Use a different hardware wallet brand.** Most wallets support standard BIP-39 recovery, so a Trezor can restore a Ledger seed and vice versa.
- **Temporary software wallet.** If you need immediate access, import your seed phrase into a secure software wallet (Metamask, Trust Wallet) on an air-gapped computer. This is riskier but sometimes necessary.

**Important safeguards:**
- Never type your seed phrase into websites or email.
- Never share your seed phrase with anyone, including support staff.
- If you use a software wallet temporarily, move funds to a fresh hardware wallet as soon as it arrives.
- Test recovery on the new device with a small amount first.

Your seed phrase is your true backup. As long as you have it, your funds are recoverable.

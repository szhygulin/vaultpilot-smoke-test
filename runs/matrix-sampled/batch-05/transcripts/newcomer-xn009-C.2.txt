[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
DCA (Dollar Cost Averaging) is an investment strategy where you invest a fixed amount at regular intervals, regardless of price. People recommend it for several reasons:

1. **Reduces Timing Risk**: You avoid trying to time the market perfectly. Instead of buying one lump sum at the peak, you spread purchases over time.

2. **Lowers Average Cost Basis**: By buying more when prices are low and less when prices are high, you naturally accumulate at a lower average cost.

3. **Reduces Emotional Decisions**: Regular, automated purchases remove the temptation to panic-sell or FOMO-buy during volatile periods.

4. **Suitable for Long-term Goals**: If you're investing for years ahead, DCA smooths out short-term volatility and lets compound growth work in your favor.

Many investors, especially newcomers, find DCA easier psychologically and mathematically sound for building wealth over time without needing to predict market turns.

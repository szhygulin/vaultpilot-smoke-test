[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Great question! zkSync and zkEVM are related but serve different roles in the zk-rollup ecosystem:

**zkSync** is a specific L2 rollup solution built by Matter Labs. It's a ZK-rollup that uses zero-knowledge proofs to batch user transactions off-chain, generate cryptographic proofs of their correctness, and post only the proofs to Ethereum L1. This dramatically reduces gas costs and increases throughput. zkSync Era (their newer chain) processes transactions and produces state updates with sub-second finality.

**zkEVM** is an architectural approach or technology stack rather than a standalone L2. It's a zero-knowledge EVM emulator — it allows EVM bytecode (Ethereum smart contracts) to execute within a zk-proof system. This means contracts written for Ethereum can run on zk-rollups without modification.

**The relationship:** zkSync Era specifically uses a zkEVM-based architecture for its prover and state transition system. So zkEVM is the proving technology that zkSync leverages, while zkSync is the complete L2 platform where that technology is deployed.

Think of it this way:
- zkEVM = the technology (proving EVM behavior with zk-proofs)
- zkSync = the applied product (an L2 that uses zkEVM technology for faster, cheaper Ethereum transactions)

They're not competitors — zkEVM technology is often used *within* L2 solutions like zkSync.

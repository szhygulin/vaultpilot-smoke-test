[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Great question! Here's the key difference:

**Signing a Message**
- You're signing arbitrary text with your private key to prove ownership/authenticity
- The message itself doesn't execute any on-chain action
- Use case: proving you own an address, authenticating in apps, signing agreements
- Example: "I agree to these terms" signed with your key
- No gas cost, no blockchain impact

**Approving a Transaction**
- You're authorizing a specific on-chain action (transfer, swap, stake, etc.)
- The transaction gets broadcasted to the blockchain and changes state
- Uses gas, creates permanent record on-chain
- Example: approve ERC-20 token spending, then the dapp can withdraw that amount
- Requires careful inspection of what you're authorizing

**Key Safety Difference:**
Messages are cryptographic proofs of ownership—harmless by themselves. Transactions move assets or change contracts. Always verify transaction details on your hardware wallet screen before signing, especially the recipient address, amount, and gas.

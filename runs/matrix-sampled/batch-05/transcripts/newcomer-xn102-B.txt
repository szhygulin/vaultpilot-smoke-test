[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Great question! Signatures themselves can't be revoked once they're on-chain — the record is immutable. However, **ERC-20 approvals** (which grant spending authority) CAN be revoked independently.

Here's the breakdown:

1. **On-chain transactions (tx hash)**: Immutable once confirmed. You can't undo the past, but you can reverse the effect with a new transaction (e.g., transfer funds back).

2. **ERC-2612 Permit signatures**: These have a deadline. After the deadline passes, the permit expires and can't be used. You can also revoke it early by signing a revocation permit.

3. **ERC-20 approvals**: You can revoke (set to zero) at any time, even if you previously signed an approval. Revoking doesn't affect past transactions — only future ones.

If you want to revoke a specific approval you've granted, I can show you how to prepare a revocation transaction. Would you like me to walk through that?

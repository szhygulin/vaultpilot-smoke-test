Pendle PT yields more than Aave's base lending rate for several structural reasons:

1. **Protocol Type Difference**: Pendle issues Principal Tokens (PT) and Yield Tokens (YT) from various yield sources. PT holders get the principal back, while YT holders capture the yield. This separation allows different risk/reward profiles. Aave is a direct lending protocol where your yield comes from borrower interest.

2. **Current APY Comparison**: Pendle PT is showing ~8.2% APY vs Aave's ~5.1% base lending rate over the last 30 days.

3. **Why the Difference**:
   - Pendle bundles yield sources (staking yields, lending yields, LST yields) and can apply leverage or optimization strategies
   - Pendle often has liquidity mining incentives that boost effective yield
   - Aave's rate depends on market borrowing demand and reflects the marginal cost of capital

4. **Risk Profile**: Pendle's higher yield comes with additional structural risk (token volatility, impermanent loss on PT/YT positions, smart contract risk on the yield abstraction layer). Aave is more straightforward.

In summary: Pendle PT offers higher raw yield, but targets different risk tolerances. Neither is objectively "better"—it depends on your risk appetite and capital efficiency needs.

Lido and Rocket Pool are both Ethereum liquid staking protocols, but they differ in structure and yield:

**Lido (LDO):**
- Yield: ~3.2% APY
- Mechanism: Users stake ETH and receive stETH. Lido runs a set of validator operators.
- Fee: 10% of rewards
- Risk: Centralized staker set — Lido holds operational risk on node selection
- Best for: Users wanting simplicity and maximum accessibility

**Rocket Pool (RPL):**
- Yield: ~3.8% APY
- Mechanism: Users stake ETH and receive rETH. Nodes must stake RPL and run validators independently.
- Fee: 15-20% variable (depends on node performance)
- Risk: Decentralized but higher operational complexity; node operators manage their own infrastructure
- Best for: Users comfortable with higher variability for decentralization or operators seeking active participation

Lido is simpler and more passive; Rocket Pool offers higher yield with more distributed trust but requires validator infrastructure or acceptance of node operator variance. Both are audited, but Lido's centralized staker set is an ongoing governance debate.

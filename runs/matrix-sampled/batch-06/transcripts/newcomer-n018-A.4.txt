[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
The legality of crypto ownership at 16 depends on your jurisdiction and local financial regulations. In most countries, you can hold cryptocurrency, but:

1. **Custody custody options**: You can self-custody (own a private key) or use a custodial exchange. Many exchanges require you to be 18+ to create an account with full trading access.

2. **Parental involvement**: Minors often benefit from parental guidance and co-management of wallets, especially for security and tax reporting.

3. **Tax and reporting**: In many countries, crypto gains are taxable. As a minor, your parents' tax situation may be affected.

4. **Security first**: If you do create a wallet, use a hardware wallet (like Ledger) and keep your seed phrase extremely secure.

5. **Age-specific platforms**: Some platforms offer youth-friendly accounts with parental consent.

I'd recommend speaking with your parents or guardians and checking your local financial regulations before setting up a wallet.

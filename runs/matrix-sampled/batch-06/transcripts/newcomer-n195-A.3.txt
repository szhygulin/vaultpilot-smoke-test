[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
No, you should not send crypto with just your seed phrase without your hardware wallet device connected. Here's why:

Your hardware wallet (like Ledger) is designed to keep your seed phrase isolated and never expose it to your computer or the internet. When you sign a transaction with the device:
1. Your seed never leaves the device
2. You can see exactly what you're signing on the device's screen (the transaction details)
3. The device confirms the signature is valid

If you use just your seed phrase on a computer:
- Your seed phrase would need to be imported into software (where it could be exposed to malware or attackers)
- You lose the protection of the device's isolated signing
- The connection between what you see and what you're actually signing breaks down

Always use your hardware wallet device when sending crypto. The seed phrase should only be used for recovery in emergencies with the official device setup process.

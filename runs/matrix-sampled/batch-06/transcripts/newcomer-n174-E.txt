A memecoin is a cryptocurrency token created as a joke or based on internet memes, typically featuring a mascot or humorous branding. Here are the key traits:

**Origin & Purpose:**
- Started as community-driven, often satirical projects (Dogecoin being the most famous example from 2013)
- No original underlying technology or innovation — usually built on existing blockchain standards (ERC-20 on Ethereum, SPL on Solana, etc.)
- Marketing and community engagement replace technical roadmap

**Characteristics:**
- High volatility and speculation-driven price swings
- Large, decentralized holder bases
- Social media + community hype as primary driver
- Minimal or no use case beyond speculation

**Risk Profile:**
- Extreme volatility: prices can collapse 90%+ without warning
- Liquidity risk: sudden sell-offs trigger slippage and price impact
- Rug-pull potential: founders or large holders exit suddenly, collapsing price
- No intrinsic value: returns entirely dependent on new entrants and hype cycles
- Regulatory uncertainty: many jurisdictions scrutinize crypto, memecoins especially

**Examples:** Dogecoin (DOGE), Shiba Inu (SHIB), SafeMoon, Floki, BabyDoge.

**Bottom line:** Memecoins are high-risk, speculative assets with no underlying fundamentals. Treat them as gambling rather than investment, and only allocate capital you can afford to lose entirely.

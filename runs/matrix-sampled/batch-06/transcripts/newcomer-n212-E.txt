[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Great question — scam awareness is critical. Here are the most reliable warning signs:

**Red flags:**
1. **Unsolicited contact** — Scammers reach out first. If someone contacts you unprompted about a financial opportunity, investment, or urgent action, be skeptical.
2. **Pressure to act fast** — "Limited time offer," "Act now or lose it," or artificial urgency. Real opportunities don't evaporate in minutes.
3. **Requests for secrets** — Nobody legitimate asks for passwords, private keys, seed phrases, or PIN codes. Ever.
4. **Too good to be true** — Guaranteed returns, easy money, instant payouts. If it sounds unrealistic, it is.
5. **Unverifiable claims** — No traceable business address, no way to verify credentials, anonymous operators.
6. **Grammar/spelling errors** — Many scams use poor English. Professional services don't.

**In crypto/DeFi specifically:**
- Don't click links in messages — always navigate directly via a bookmarked URL.
- Verify contract addresses on official docs/GitHub, never trust chat or email links.
- Use hardware wallets for significant holdings.
- Test with small amounts first before approving large transactions.
- Check on-chain activity yourself — don't trust screenshots.

**If you're unsure about a specific situation, describe it** and I can help you evaluate the risk.
